HAPPY RAKSHA BANDHAN

HISTORY OF RAKSHA BANDHAN

The history of Raksha Bandhan is steeped in mythological tales and historical anecdotes that underscore the bond of protection and care between siblings. One of the most revered stories is that of Lord Krishna and Draupadi. In the epic Mahabharata, Draupadi tore a strip of her sari and tied it around Krishna's wrist to staunch a bleeding wound. Touched by her gesture, Krishna promised to protect her always.

CELEBRATIONS

Raksha Bandhan, sisters prepare meticulously by choosing rakhis that range from traditional threads to intricately designed ones, often embellished with beads, motifs, and symbols of protection. The ceremony begins with prayers and aarti, followed by the sister tying the rakhi around her brother's wrist, symbolising her love and his commitment to protect her. Brothers, in turn, offer gifts and promise to safeguard their sisters' well-being throughout their lives. This exchange is often accompanied by familial gatherings, feasting on sweets like laddoos and barfis, and sharing joyous moments.
